# ROAD TRIP OPTION 2: The Hidden Europe Route
## Austria → Slovenia → Northeastern Italy → Paris

**Dates:** Sunday June 28 – Wednesday July 1, 2026 (4 days, extendable)  
**Group:** 2 couples (4 people)  
**Starting Point:** Mondsee, Austria  
**Ending Point:** Paris, France (via Venice, Ljubljana, or Trieste)  
**Total Distance:** ~550-750 km driving + train/flight to Paris  
**Theme:** Hidden gems, Julian Alps, emerald rivers, less crowded, authentic

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This alternative eastern route takes you from Mondsee **southeast into Slovenia**, one of Europe's most underrated destinations. You'll drive the spectacular **Vršič Pass** (50 hairpin bends), explore the emerald **Soča River Valley**, discover charming **Ljubljana**, and emerge through northeastern Italy or Croatia's Istrian Peninsula. This route offers a more adventurous, authentic experience away from the tourist crowds of the Dolomites.

**Why Choose This Route:**
- **Uncrowded:** Slovenia remains one of Europe's best-kept secrets
- **Spectacular driving:** Vršič Pass rivals any Alpine road
- **Unique scenery:** Emerald Soča River, Julian Alps, Karst landscape
- **Cultural diversity:** Austrian, Slavic, and Italian influences
- **Better value:** Lower costs than Italy for accommodations and dining
- **Adventure options:** Rafting, hiking, canyoning in Soča Valley

## DETAILED ITINERARY

### **DAY 1: SUNDAY JUNE 28 - Mondsee to Lake Bled, Slovenia**
**Theme:** Cross-Border Journey to the Fairy-Tale Lake  
**Driving:** ~180 km, 2.5-3 hours  
**Border Crossing:** Austria → Slovenia at Bleiburg/Prevalje  
**Overnight:** Lake Bled, Slovenia

#### Morning: Departure from Mondsee (8:00 AM)

**Route:** A1 → E61 through Klagenfurt, Austria
- Take A1 south from Mondsee
- Continue past Salzburg toward Klagenfurt
- **Vignette required:** Buy Austrian vignette at border if not already purchased (€9.90)

**10:00 AM - Faaker See (Austria, just before border)**
- **Quick stop:** Beautiful alpine lake, less crowded than Austrian lakes
- **Finkenstein Castle** ruins overlooking the lake
- Coffee break at lakeside café

**11:00 AM - Cross into Slovenia**
- Border crossing near **Bleiburg/Prevalje**
- **Schengen border** - no stops, just drive through
- Buy **Slovenian vignette** immediately at gas station (€15 for 7 days)
- **Warning:** Heavy fines (€300+) for driving without vignette

**11:30 AM - Continue toward Lake Bled**
- Drive through **Slovenian Carinthia**
- Rolling green hills, charming villages

#### Afternoon: Arrive Lake Bled (12:30 PM)

**Lake Bled - Slovenia's Crown Jewel**
- **The iconic scene:** Church-topped island, cliff-top castle, Julian Alps backdrop
- **Far less crowded than** Hallstatt or Lake Como
- **More affordable** than Italian lakes

**Check into Hotel:**

#### Afternoon Activities (2:00 PM - 6:00 PM):

**1. Walk the Lake Circuit (2 hours, 6 km)**
- Flat, easy walk around the entire lake
- Best photo spots:
  - **Mala Osojnica viewpoint** (short hike for iconic photo)
  - **Vila Zlatorog** area for castle + island views
  - **Benches along the north shore**

**2. Bled Castle (1.5 hours)**
- Perched 130m above the lake
- **Admission:** €15
- Museum of Bled's history
- **Wine cellar:** Sample Slovenian wines
- **Castle terrace restaurant:** Coffee with panoramic views

**3. Vintgar Gorge (Optional, 4 km away)**
- Spectacular 1.6 km wooden walkway through gorge
- **Crystal-clear turquoise water**, waterfalls
- **Admission:** €10
- **Time needed:** 1.5 hours
- **Note:** Very popular - go early morning next day if interested

#### Evening in Bled:
- **Sunset views** from the lakefront
- **Dinner at Hotel Triglav restaurant** or **Slaščičarna Šmon**
- **Must try:** **Bled Cream Cake** (kremšnita) - the original at Park Café
- **Evening walk** along the illuminated lake

### **DAY 2: MONDAY JUNE 29 - Lake Bled → Soča Valley via Vršič Pass**
**Theme:** The Spectacular Mountain Crossing  
**Driving:** ~80 km, 2-3 hours (slow due to mountain road)  
**Overnight:** Bovec or Kobarid, Slovenia

#### Morning: The Vršič Pass Drive (9:00 AM - 1:00 PM)

**This is the highlight of the entire trip.**

**The Vršič Pass (1,611m / 5,285 ft)**
- **Slovenia's highest mountain pass**
- **50 hairpin bends** (24 ascending, 26 descending)
- Built by **10,000-12,000 Russian POWs** during WWI
- Known as the **"Russian Road"**

**Route:** Lake Bled → Kranjska Gora → Vršič Pass → Trenta → Soča Valley

**Preparation:**
- Fill up with fuel in Bled (last chance for 80 km)
- Buy water and snacks
- Check weather - road closes in heavy snow/winter

**Key Stops:**

**9:30 AM - Kranjska Gora**
- Famous ski resort town
- **Jasna Lake** - turquoise alpine lake, perfect for photos
- Coffee stop before the climb

**10:30 AM - Start Vršič Pass Ascent**
- The road begins winding upward
- Speed limit: 30-50 km/h
- **Watch for:** Cyclists, motorcyclists, oncoming traffic on tight turns

**11:30 AM - Russian Orthodox Chapel**
- **Stop #1:** Wooden chapel built by Russian prisoners (1916-1917)
- Memorial to the hundreds who died in avalanches
- Poignant reminder of the road's history
- **Turn #8** on the ascent

**12:00 PM - Tičar Lodge (1,620m)**
- **Highest mountain hut** on the pass
- Restaurant with Slovenian mountain food
- **Restroom break**
- Views of the Julian Alps

**12:30 PM - Vršič Pass Summit**
- **Erection to the builders**
- Panoramic views
- Photo opportunity
- Start descent (26 hairpins down)

#### Afternoon: Soča Valley (1:00 PM - Evening)

**The Emerald Soča River**
- **Famous for:** Otherworldly emerald-green color
- So clear you can see riverbed 10+ meters down
- Featured in **Disney's Chronicles of Narnia: Prince Caspian**
- One of Europe's most beautiful rivers

**Route Options After Vršič:**

**Option A: Bovec (Adventure Capital)**
- **White-water rafting** on Soča (Class II-IV rapids)
- **Zip-lining** (longest in Europe - 3.2 km)
- **Canyoning, paragliding, kayaking**
- **Town atmosphere:** Lively, restaurants, cafés

**Option B: Kobarid (History & Culture)**
- **Kobarid Museum** - exceptional WWI Isonzo Front museum
  - Winner of European Museum Award
  - 12 battles fought here (1915-1917)
  - Hemingway's "A Farewell to Arms" setting
- **Kozjak Waterfall** - 15m waterfall in hidden amphitheater
- **More relaxed, historic village**

**Recommended Schedule:**

**2:00 PM - Arrive Bovec**
- Check into hotel
- **Lunch:** Restaurant Dobra Vila or Gostilna Rombon

**3:30 PM - Activity Choice:**
- **Rafting:** 2-3 hour trip on Soča (€50-70/person)
- **Easy hike:** Soča River Trail (flat, 2 hours)
- **Relaxation:** Enjoy the town, river views

**OR**

**2:30 PM - Drive to Kobarid (15 min from Bovec)**
- Visit **Kobarid Museum** (1.5 hours)
- **Kozjak Waterfall** walk (45 minutes)
- Explore village

#### Overnight in Soča Valley:

| **Hotel Bovec** | €90-120 | Central, comfortable, spa |

**Dinner:**

### **DAY 3: TUESDAY JUNE 30 - Soča Valley → Ljubljana**
**Theme:** Capital City Discovery  
**Driving:** ~110 km, 1.5 hours (or scenic route via Idrija: 2.5 hours)  
**Overnight:** Ljubljana, Slovenia

#### Morning: Soča Valley → Ljubljana

**Option 1: Direct Route (A2 Motorway)**
- Fastest option (1.5 hours)
- Pay toll with vignette
- Arrive in Ljubljana by 11 AM

**Option 2: Scenic Route via Idrija (RECOMMENDED)**
- Add 1 hour but see UNESCO heritage site
- Route: Kobarid → Idrija → Ljubljana

**9:00 AM - Depart Soča Valley**

**10:30 AM - Idrija (if taking scenic route)**
- **UNESCO World Heritage site**
- **Anthony's Shaft** - second largest mercury mine in world
- **Idrija Lace** - intricate bobbin lace tradition
- **Žlikrofi** - local pasta specialty (dumplings with potato filling)
- **Idrija War Museum** - interesting local history
- **Time:** 1 hour visit

**12:00 PM - Continue to Ljubljana**

**1:00 PM - Arrive Ljubljana**
- Check into hotel
- **Park in:** Hotel garage, or public parking (€15-20/day)
- Ljubljana city center is **car-free**

#### Afternoon: Exploring Ljubljana (2:00 PM - 7:00 PM)

**Ljubljana - Europe's Most Underrated Capital**
- Population: 300,000 (intimate size)
- **Completely car-free city center**
- Green Capital of Europe 2016
- Dragon symbolism everywhere

**Walking Tour Route (3 hours):**

**1. Ljubljana Castle (1.5 hours)**
- **Funicular railway** up (€4) or 15-minute walk
- **Viewing tower** - 360° city views
- **History museum**
- **Gostilna na Gradu** restaurant (upscale Slovenian)
- **Castle courtyard** for photos

**2. Old Town Walk (1 hour)**
- **Prešeren Square** - heart of city, pink Franciscan Church
- **Triple Bridge** (Tromostovje) - iconic Plečnik architecture
- **Dragon Bridge** - four dragons guarding the city
- **Central Market** - food market + colonnade (open until 2 PM)
- **Cobbler's Bridge** - oldest bridge

**3. Riverside Cafés (evening)**
- **Ljubljanica River** lined with outdoor cafés
- **Stritarjeva Street** - pedestrian zone
- **Aperitivo culture** like Italy but more relaxed

#### Evening in Ljubljana:

**Dinner Options:**
| Restaurant | Style | Notes |

**Evening Activities:**
- **Evening cruise** on Ljubljanica River (€10)
- **Metelkova Art District** - alternative culture, street art (like Copenhagen's Christiania)
- **Live music** at Cankarjevo Nabrežje (riverside)

**Cons:**
- Long journey
- Requires connections
- Arrives late evening

**Direct flight to Paris**  
**Best for:** Time-conscious travelers

**Flights:**
- **Air France:** Ljubljana (LJU) → Paris CDG
- **EasyJet:** Ljubljana (LJU) → Paris ORY
- **Duration:** 2 hours
- **Cost:** €80-200

**Schedule:**
- **Morning flight:** Depart 10:00 AM, arrive 12:00 PM
- **Afternoon in Paris** for arrival

**Pros:**
- Fastest option
- Morning departure
- Full day in Paris upon arrival

**Cons:**
- Need to return rental car at airport
- Airport security/wait times
- Less scenic

- Collect luggage

**Train Options:**

|-----------|---------|----------|-------|

## EXTENSION OPTIONS

### **Extension A: Trieste & Friuli, Italy (1-2 days)**

**Route:** Ljubljana → Trieste (1.5 hours) → Venice area

**Day 4-5: Trieste**
- **Unique atmosphere:** Italy's most un-Italian city
- **Habsburg heritage:** Grand architecture, Viennese coffee culture
- **Miramare Castle:** Spectacular cliff-top castle
- **Grotta Gigante:** Enormous cave nearby
- **Piazza Unità d'Italia:** Europe's largest sea-facing square

**Day 6:** Train Trieste → Venice → Paris

### **Extension B: Istria, Croatia (2-3 days)**

**Route:** Ljubljana → Rovinj, Croatia (2.5 hours)

**Day 4-6: Istrian Peninsula**
- **Rovinj:** Romantic Venetian-style coastal town
- **Pula:** Roman amphitheater (6th largest in world)
- **Motovun:** Tuscany-like hill town with truffles
- **Gastronomy:** Truffles, olive oil, wine (Malvasia)

**Return:** Drive to Venice or Ljubljana for Paris connection

### **Extension C: More Slovenia (1-2 days)**

**Day 4-5: Postojna Caves & Piran**
- **Postojna Cave:** Europe's most visited show cave (train inside!)
- **Predjama Castle:** Castle built into cave mouth
- **Piran:** Venetian-style coastal town on Adriatic

**Day 6:** Return to Ljubljana or Venice for Paris

## PROS & CONS

### ✅ PROS
- **Uncrowded destinations** - authentic experiences
- **Vršič Pass** - spectacular driving experience
- **Better value** - lower costs for hotels/food
- **Emerald Soča River** - unique natural wonder
- **Ljubljana** - charming, relaxed capital
- **Less touristy** - feel like a traveler, not a tourist
- **Excellent English** spoken in Slovenia
- **Cultural variety** - Slavic, Germanic, Italian influences

### ❌ CONS
- **Vršič Pass** - challenging mountain driving
- **Less famous** - friends may not recognize photos
- **Return logistics** - fewer direct options to Paris
- **Smaller infrastructure** - fewer luxury hotels
- **Road conditions** - Vršič can close in bad weather
- **One-way rental** - still expensive across borders

## FINAL RECOMMENDATION

**Choose This Eastern Route If:**
- You want to avoid tourist crowds
- You enjoy discovering hidden gems
- You're comfortable with mountain driving (Vršič Pass)
- You value authentic experiences over famous landmarks
- You want better value for money
- You're interested in outdoor activities (rafting, hiking)
- You want a unique travel story

**Skip This Route If:**
- You want the "postcard" famous views (Dolomites)
- You're nervous about mountain driving
- You need luxury hotel chains (limited in Slovenia)
- You prefer well-established tourist infrastructure
