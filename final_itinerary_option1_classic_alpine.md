# ROAD TRIP OPTION 1: The Classic Alpine Route
## Austria → Dolomites → Northern Italy → Paris

**Dates:** Sunday June 28 – Wednesday July 1, 2026 (4 days, extendable to Thursday/Friday)  
**Group:** 2 couples (4 people)  
**Starting Point:** Mondsee, Austria  
**Ending Point:** Venice, Italy → Paris, France  
**Total Distance:** ~450 km driving + train to Paris  
**Theme:** Alpine scenery, Mozart's lakes, mountain passes, Venice finale

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This classic western route takes you from the Austrian Salzkammergut lakes region, through **Wolfgangsee** (where Mozart's sister Nannerl lived), across the dramatic **Grossglockner High Alpine Road** (2,504m), through the stunning **Dolomites UNESCO World Heritage Site**, and concludes with **2 nights in Venice** before your return to Paris. You'll experience Alpine grandeur and end in one of the world's most romantic cities.

**Why Choose This Route:**
- **Mozart connection:** Visit Wolfgangsee where Nannerl Mozart lived
- **Epic driving:** Grossglockner High Alpine Road with 36 hairpin turns
- **Dolomites:** UNESCO peaks and alpine lakes
- **Venice finale:** End in La Serenissima, not a business city
- **Direct trains:** Venice to Paris in under 7 hours
- **Perfect mix:** Nature, culture, and romance

## DETAILED ITINERARY

### **DAY 1: SUNDAY JUNE 28 - Mondsee to Heiligenblut**
**Theme:** Austrian Lakes & Alpine Pass Crossing  
**Driving:** ~180 km, 5-6 hours with stops  
**Overnight:** Heiligenblut am Großglockner, Austria

#### Morning: Mondsee → Wolfgangsee → Bad Ischl (2 hours)

**8:00 AM - Depart Mondsee**
- Pick up rental cars (if applicable)
- Quick breakfast at lakeside café

**8:30 AM - 10:30 AM: Wolfgangsee & St. Wolfgang**
- **Mozart Connection:** Nannerl Mozart (Wolfgang's older sister) lived here
- **Pfarrkirche St. Wolfgang:** Pilgrimage church with famous Michael Pacher altarpiece
- **Schafbergbahn:** Cogwheel railway to 1,783m peak (1 hour round trip, €40)
- **Weisses Rössl:** Historic lakeside hotel (inspired the operetta)
- **Lakeside promenade walk:** 30 minutes along the water
- **Photo spot:** Classic view of church spire from lake
- **Coffee:** Café Fürberg lakeside terrace

**10:30 AM - 11:00 AM: Drive to Bad Ischl (20 min)**

**11:00 AM - 12:30 PM: Bad Ischl - Coffee & Culture**
- **Café Zauner** (est. 1832) - Emperor Franz Joseph's favorite
  - Try the Zaunerstollen (local pastry)
  - Historic interior, reasonable prices
- **Kaiservilla** (optional, 1 hour) - Imperial summer residence
- Stroll the Esplanade along the Traun River

#### Afternoon: The Grossglockner High Alpine Road (3-4 hours)

**1:00 PM - Depart Bad Ischl toward Grossglockner**
- Route: B311 → B158 → B311 to Bruck/Ferleiten
- **Important:** Buy vignette (€9.90, 10 days) if not already purchased

**2:00 PM - Ferleiten Toll Plaza**
- **Toll:** €40-45 per vehicle (credit card accepted)
- Check weather - road closes in poor conditions
- Fill up with fuel (last chance before mountain)

**2:30 PM - 6:00 PM: The Drive of a Lifetime**

| Stop | Elevation | Time | Highlights |
|------|-----------|------|------------|
| **Ferleiten** | 1,100m | 2:00 PM | Entry point, exhibition |
| **Edelweißspitze** | 2,571m | 2:45 PM | Highest point by car, 360° views, 1.5 km detour |
| **Fuscher Törl** | 2,428m | 3:30 PM | View of Grossglockner peak (3,798m) |
| **Hochtor Pass** | 2,504m | 4:00 PM | The summit, Austria's highest paved road |
| **Kaiser-Franz-Josefs-Höhe** | 2,369m | 4:30 PM | Pasterze Glacier viewpoint, visitor center, restaurant |

**Scenic Driving Notes:**
- **36 hairpin turns** in total
- Speed limit: 50 km/h (strictly enforced)
- Stop at designated viewpoints only
- Watch for marmots, ibex, golden eagles
- **The Pasterze Glacier** - Austria's longest glacier (8 km)

**6:00 PM - Arrive Heiligenblut**
- Check into hotel
- Village stroll to stretch legs
- Dinner at hotel restaurant or Gasthof

#### Overnight: Heiligenblut am Großglockner

**Village Highlights:**

#### Morning: Depart Cortina for Venice (8:00 AM)

**Route:** SS51 → A27 → A4 toward Venice
- **Driving:** 2 hours (160 km)

**10:00 AM - Arrive Venice**

#### Morning/Afternoon: Explore Venice (10:00 AM - 5:00 PM)

**Getting Around:**
- **Vaporetto** (water bus) - buy 1-day pass (€25) or single tickets (€9.50)
- **Walking:** Venice is compact and walkable (but easy to get lost!)
- **Water taxi:** Expensive but convenient (€70-100 per trip)

**Must-See Highlights:**

**1. St. Mark's Square (Piazza San Marco) - 2 hours**
- **St. Mark's Basilica** (free, book ahead online to skip line)
  - Byzantine architecture, golden mosaics
  - Pala d'Oro altarpiece (€5 extra)
  - Terrace views (€10 extra)
- **Doge's Palace** (€30, includes Correr Museum)
  - Gothic architecture
  - Bridge of Sighs
  - Secret itineraries tour (€28 extra, book ahead)
- **St. Mark's Campanile** (€10) - elevator to top for panoramic views
- **Caffè Florian** (est. 1720) - historic café for espresso (expensive but iconic)

**2. Rialto Bridge & Market - 1.5 hours**
- **Rialto Bridge** - iconic stone bridge, shops on bridge
- **Rialto Market** (Mon-Sat morning) - fish and produce market
- **Erbaria** - lively square for spritz

**3. Walking Tour - 2 hours**
- Get purposefully lost in the backstreets
- Cross tiny bridges over quiet canals
- **Campo Santa Margherita** - local student hangout
- **Academy Bridge (Ponte dell'Accademia)** - best view of Grand Canal
- **Rialto to San Marco walk** - follow yellow signs or use GPS

**4. Gondola Ride (Optional - €80-120 for 30 min)**
- Cheaper alternative: **Traghetto** (€2) - gondola ferry across Grand Canal
- Best route: Rialto to San Marco area

## VENICE PRACTICAL TIPS

### Getting There/Departing
- **Piazzale Roma:** Car return and bus terminal
- **Santa Lucia Station:** Trains to Paris (book ahead!)
- **Marco Polo Airport:** Flights available

### Avoiding Crowds
- **Early morning (8-10 AM):** St. Mark's area is quiet
- **Evening (after 6 PM):** Day-trippers leave
- **Get lost on purpose:** Backstreets are empty even midday

### Money-Saving Tips
- **Free sights:** Walk across Rialto Bridge, St. Mark's Square (outside), wander streets
- **Cicchetti bars:** €2-4 per snack + €3-5 for wine
- **Water:** Drink from public fountains (safe and free)
- **Skip gondola:** Take traghetto (€2) instead

### What to Avoid
- ❌ Eating right next to St. Mark's Square (overpriced, touristy)
- ❌ Buying "Murano glass" from street vendors (usually fake)
- ❌ Feeding pigeons (illegal, €50+ fines)
- ❌ Sitting on church steps (guards will move you)
